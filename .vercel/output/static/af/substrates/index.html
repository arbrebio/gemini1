Coco Peat, Coir Fibre & Coconut Substrate: Complete Guide

Coco-based substrates are the most suitable soilless growing media for African conditions — affordable, sustainable and high-performing.

Coco Peat

Top soilless substrate: water retention 8-9× its weight, pH 5.5-6.8, 100% natural. Ideal for tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, strawberries.

Coconut Coir Fibre

Improves tropical soil structure — increases clay soil porosity, drainage and is an excellent mulch. Lifespan 3-5 years.

Perlite & Vermiculite

Mineral complements: perlite improves aeration, vermiculite retains nutrients. Optimal mix: 70% coco + 30% perlite.

FAQ: Coco Substrates for African Agriculture

Why use coco peat instead of soil in Africa?

Coco peat offers: no weeds or pathogens, 8× higher water retention than sandy soils, stable controllable pH, and 2-3× higher yields for vegetable crops.

How to prepare coco peat for growing?

1) Soak 1kg brick in 5L water (expands to 8-9L). 2) Rinse if EC>1.5 mS/cm. 3) Adjust pH to 5.5-6.5. 4) Optionally mix with 20-30% perlite. We supply ready-to-use substrate.

How much does coco peat cost in Côte d'Ivoire?

We offer dehydrated bricks (650g) from 1,500 FCFA, bulk bags (50kg) from 25,000 FCFA, and ready-to-use grow bags. Contact us for volume pricing.

What is the lifespan of coco peat as a substrate?

Well-managed coco peat lasts 2-3 growing cycles (12-18 months) with rinsing, sterilisation, and EC/pH monitoring. Spent substrate makes excellent organic soil amendment.

Does Arbre Bio Africa deliver substrates across Africa?

Yes, we deliver coco peat, coir fibre, perlite and vermiculite across West Africa (Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Ghana, Burkina Faso, Mali, Togo, Benin) with agronomic training and follow-up.
